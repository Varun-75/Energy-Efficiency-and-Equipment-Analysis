# ⚡ Energy Efficiency & Equipment Performance Analytics

## 📌 Overview
This project focuses on analyzing industrial equipment performance and energy consumption using a synthetic dataset modeled on real-world refinery operations.

The objective is to identify:
- Energy-intensive equipment  
- Load patterns across time  
- High-risk operating conditions  
- Opportunities for efficiency improvement  

---

## 🏭 Problem Statement
Industrial facilities operate under varying load conditions, which can lead to:
- Increased energy consumption  
- Thermal stress on equipment  
- Unexpected downtime  

This project aims to simulate and analyze such conditions to provide data-driven insights for operational optimization and reliability improvement.

---

## 📊 Dataset
A synthetic dataset was generated using Python to mimic real industrial systems, including:

- Multiple equipment types (Motor, Pump, Compressor)  
- Time-series data (hourly over 30 days)  
- Operational parameters:
  - Load (%)  
  - Power (kW)  
  - Voltage (V)  
  - Current (A)  
  - Temperature (°C)  
  - Downtime (minutes)  

The dataset incorporates:
- Daily load cycles  
- Equipment-specific behavior  
- Failure conditions based on high load and temperature  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)  
- Power BI (Dashboard & Visualization)  
- Excel (Data handling)

---

## 📈 Analysis Performed

### 🔹 Energy Consumption Analysis
- Identified equipment with highest energy usage  
- Compared consumption across assets  

### 🔹 Load Pattern Analysis
- Analyzed hourly load trends  
- Identified peak and off-peak operating periods  

### 🔹 Correlation Analysis
- Strong relationship observed between load and temperature  
- Thermal stress identified as a key factor in equipment performance  

### 🔹 Downtime Analysis
- Evaluated equipment reliability  
- Identified assets with higher failure frequency  

### 🔹 High-Risk Condition Detection
- Defined risk conditions:
  - Load > 85%  
  - Temperature > 80°C  
- Measured frequency of operation under stress  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Compressor systems showed the highest energy consumption and downtime, making them prime candidates for optimization  
- Peak load occurs during early operational hours, indicating concentrated system usage  
- Strong correlation between load and temperature highlights thermal stress as a major reliability factor  
- Equipment operating under high load and temperature conditions shows increased failure probability  

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- Implement predictive maintenance based on temperature thresholds  
- Optimize load distribution to reduce peak stress  
- Shift non-critical operations to off-peak hours  
- Improve cooling and monitoring systems for high-load equipment
